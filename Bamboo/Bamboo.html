SCIENTIFIC NAME
Dracaena Sanderiana
COMMON NAME
Lucky Bamboo
HEIGHT
10-15 cm
WIDTH
4-10 cm
LEAVES COLOR
Green
FLOWERS COLOR
No flower

Botanical Characteristics:

  • Belongs to the Dracaena genus, often called "Lucky Bamboo," though not a true bamboo.
  • Features straight, hollow, jointed stems with bright green leaves.
  • Can be grown in water or soil, making it adaptable for indoor spaces.
  • Grows well in low to medium light, making it suitable for offices and homes.

Varieties:

  • Lucky Bamboo (Dracaena sanderiana) — most common indoor variety.
  • Golden Bamboo — has yellowish-green stems.
  • Curly or Spiral Bamboo — stems trained into spiral shapes, often used in decorative arrangements.
  • Buddha Bamboo — features thick stems and is associated with good luck and prosperity.

Benefits:

  • Believed to bring good luck, prosperity, and positive energy as per Feng Shui.
  • Helps purify indoor air by absorbing toxins.
  • Easy to grow and maintain, making it ideal for beginners.
  • Adds a natural, aesthetic touch to homes and workspaces.

Care Instructions:

  • Keep in filtered water, changing water every 7–10 days, or plant in well-draining soil.
  • Avoid direct sunlight; prefers indirect bright light.
  • Use distilled or filtered water as tap water chemicals can harm the plant.
  • Trim yellow leaves or stems to promote healthy growth.
