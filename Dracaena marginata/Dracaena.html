Botanical Characteristics:

  • Scientific Name: Dracaena marginata, also known as Madagascar Dragon Tree.
  • appearance: Slender, woody stems with long, narrow, pointed leaves edged in red or purple.
  • growth: Can grow up to 6%E2%80%9310 feet indoors; slow-growing and suitable as a decorative floor plant.
  • type: Evergreen, drought-tolerant, and native to Madagascar.

Varieties:

  • tricolor: Features green leaves with red edges and a cream-colored stripe in the center.
  • colorama: Has more pronounced pinkish-red tones and dense foliage.
  • bicolor: Displays green leaves with red edges but no cream stripe.

Benefits:

  • Air Purifying: Removes toxins like formaldehyde and benzene from indoor air (NASA study).
  • Low Maintenance: Thrives in low-light conditions and infrequent watering.
  • Aesthetic Appeal: Adds a modern, tropical look to home or office interiors.
  • Stress Relief: Indoor plants have been linked to improved mood and productivity.

Care Instructions:

  • Light: Prefers bright, indirect sunlight; tolerates low light.
  • Water: Allow soil to dry slightly between watering; avoid overwatering.
  • Humidity & Temp: Average room humidity and temperatures between 18–27°C are ideal.
  • Soil: Use well-draining potting mix; repot every 2–3 years.
