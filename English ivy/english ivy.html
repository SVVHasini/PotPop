English Ivy

6-9 meters

Dark green
FLOWERS COLOR
Greenish white and greenish yellow

Botanical Characteristics:

  • Scientific name: Hedera helix, commonly known as English Ivy.
  • It is an evergreen, climbing or trailing vine with attractive lobed leaves.
  • Leaves are typically dark green with lighter veins, but may vary depending on the variety.
  • It grows using aerial rootlets that cling to surfaces like walls or pots.

Varieties:

  • Glacier Ivy – Small leaves with gray-green centers and creamy-white edges.
  • Needlepoint Ivy – Has deeply lobed, narrow leaves resembling star shapes.
  • Gold Child Ivy – Features green leaves with yellow or golden edges.
  • Eva Ivy – Compact variety with gray-green leaves edged in cream.

Benefits:

  • Acts as a natural air purifier, removing pollutants like formaldehyde.
  • Provides aesthetic value with its trailing vines, suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
  • Helps in improving indoor humidity levels slightly.
  • Can reduce mold in damp indoor spaces if properly maintained.

Care Instructions:

  • Thrives in bright, indirect light, but tolerates low light conditions.
  • Keep soil slightly moist, but avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.
  • Prefers cooler indoor temperatures around 15°C to 21°C (59°F to 70°F).
  • Occasional misting helps maintain humidity and keeps pests like spider mites away.
