SCIENTIFIC NAME
Dypsis Lutescens
COMMON NAME
Areca palm plant
HEIGHT
5-8 feet
WIDTH
10-20 feet
LEAVES COLOR
Green with yellow and orange tinted

Botanical Characteristics:

  • Scientific Name: Dypsis lutescens, commonly known as Areca Palm or Butterfly Palm.
  • It is a tropical, evergreen palm with feathery, arching fronds.
  • Typically grows in clumps with smooth, yellow-green stems resembling bamboo.
  • Mature plants can reach a height of 6 to 12 feet indoors.

Varieties:

  • Dypsis lutescens (Golden Cane Palm) — most popular indoor variety.
  • Chrysalidocarpus lutescens — older botanical classification, still commonly used in nurseries.
  • Limited recognized varieties, but leaf size and growth pattern may vary based on growing conditions.

Benefits:

  • Acts as a natural air purifier by removing toxins like formaldehyde and carbon monoxide.
  • Adds aesthetic appeal with lush, tropical foliage, making spaces look vibrant.
  • Increases indoor humidity, which can help reduce dry skin and respiratory issues.
  • Non-toxic to pets, making it a safe houseplant for homes with cats and dogs.

Care Instructions:

  • Requires bright, indirect sunlight; avoid harsh direct sun.
  • Water moderately—keep the soil slightly moist but not soggy.
  • Prefers well-drained soil and occasional misting to maintain humidity.
  • Prune dead or yellowing fronds to encourage healthy growth.
