Botanical Characteristics:

  • Orchids belong to the family Orchidaceae, one of the largest flowering plant families.
  • They are known for their unique and symmetrical flowers, often with intricate patterns.
  • Orchids can be epiphytic (growing on trees) or terrestrial (growing in soil).
  • They have thick aerial roots, which help them absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Varieties:

  • Phalaenopsis (Moth Orchids) – Popular for their long-lasting blooms and ease of care.
  • Dendrobium – Known for their tall canes and abundant flowers.
  • Cattleya – Famous for their large, fragrant, and colorful flowers.
  • Vanda – Valued for vibrant colors and striking flower patterns.

Benefits:

  • Enhance indoor aesthetics with their exotic and elegant flowers.
  • Some varieties help improve indoor air quality by absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen.
  • Orchids symbolize love, beauty, and strength, making them a popular gift choice.
  • Their blooms can last several weeks, providing long-lasting decoration.

Care Instructions:

  • Place them in indirect sunlight; avoid direct harsh rays.
  • Water once a week, allowing roots to dry slightly between watering.
  • Maintain high humidity and good air circulation.
  • Use a well-draining orchid potting mix, typically bark or sphagnum moss-based.
