SCIENTIFIC NAME
Aloe Barbedensis Miller
COMMON NAME
Aloe vera
HEIGHT
1 to 2 feet
WIDTH
6 to 12 inches
LEAVES COLOR
grey-green or blue-green

Botanical Characteristics:

  • Succulent Structure: Thick, fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that store water, ideal for arid conditions.
  • Leaf Features: Leaves are green to grey-green with serrated edges and sometimes white speckles or bands.
  • Flowering: Produces tall spikes of tubular yellow, orange, or red flowers, especially when grown in favorable conditions.
  • Water Storage Ability: Naturally adapted to dry environments due to water-retaining leaf tissues.

Varieties:

  • Aloe barbadensis miller (True Aloe): The most common medicinal variety, known for its clear, healing gel.
  • Aloe arborescens (Torch Aloe): A larger species with vibrant red-orange flowers, often used for hedges.
  • Aloe juvenna (Tiger Tooth Aloe): Small, ornamental aloe with triangular leaves edged by tiny teeth, suitable for pots.
  • Aloe aristata (Lace Aloe): A compact, decorative variety with patterned, white-flecked leaves and soft spines.

Benefits:

  • Gel soothes burns, cuts, insect bites, and sunburn; promotes faster healing.
  • Commonly added to moisturizers, shampoos, soaps, and sunscreens for its hydrating properties.
  • Helps remove indoor air pollutants like formaldehyde and benzene, improving air quality.

Care Instructions:

  • Light Requirements: Prefers bright, indirect sunlight; tolerates some direct sun with gradual exposure.
  • Soil and Potting: Needs well-draining, sandy or cactus soil; terra cotta pots are ideal for better air circulation.
  • Watering: Water thoroughly only when the soil is completely dry; overwatering leads to root rot.
