Know about me

SCIENTIFIC NAME
Tradescantia
COMMON NAME
Hairy Stem Spiderwort
HEIGHT
1 feet
WIDTH
1 feet
LEAVES COLOR
green to purple
FLOWERS COLOR
bright pink with white mid line

Botanical Characteristics:

  • Belongs to the Commelinaceae family, commonly known as Spiderwort.
  • Features elongated, fleshy leaves that are often green, purple, or variegated.
  • Produces small, three-petaled flowers—usually pink, purple, or white.

Varieties:

  • Tradescantia zebrina – Known for its purple and silver striped leaves.
  • Tradescantia pallida (Purple Heart) – Deep purple leaves and vibrant violet flowers.
  • Tradescantia fluminensis – Has shiny green leaves, sometimes variegated with white.

Benefits:

  • Acts as an effective air-purifying plant, improving indoor air quality.
  • Its colorful foliage adds a decorative touch to homes and offices.
  • Easy to propagate, making it ideal for sharing or expanding collections.

Care Instructions:

  • Thrives in bright, indirect sunlight; too much shade dulls leaf color.
  • Water moderately; keep soil lightly moist, but avoid overwatering.
  • Prefers well-draining soil and benefits from occasional pruning to encourage bushiness.
