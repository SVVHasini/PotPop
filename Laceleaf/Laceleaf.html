SCIENTIFIC NAME
Anthurium
COMMON NAME
Laceleaf
HEIGHT
3 feet
WIDTH
10-12 inches
LEAVES COLOR
Dark green
FLOWERS COLOR
red,pink,white

Botanical Characteristics:

  • Laceleaf (Anthurium andraeanum) is a tropical perennial plant known for its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, waxy spathes (often mistaken as flowers).
  • The actual flower is the spadix — a spike at the center of the spathe.
  • It belongs to the Araceae family and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Varieties:

  • Anthurium andraeanum – most common, known for red spathes.
  • Anthurium scherzerianum – features a curled spadix and orange to red spathes.
  • Hybrids are available in various spathe colors like white, pink, purple, and green.

Benefits:

  • Acts as a natural air purifier by removing toxins like formaldehyde and ammonia.
  • Adds an exotic, decorative touch to indoor spaces.
  • Its long-lasting blooms make it a popular gift plant.

Care Instructions:

  • Prefers bright, indirect light and temperatures between 18–25°C.
  • Keep soil moist but not soggy; use a well-draining potting mix.
  • Mist regularly or use a humidifier to maintain high humidity.
  • Fertilize monthly during growing seasons with a balanced houseplant fertilizer.
