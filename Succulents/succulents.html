Botanical Characteristics:

  • Succulents are drought-resistant plants that store water in their thick, fleshy leaves, stems, or roots.
  • They often have a waxy coating or spines to reduce water loss.
  • Most succulents thrive in arid or semi-arid climates.

Varieties:

  • Popular types include Aloe Vera, Echeveria, Jade Plant (Crassula ovata), and Sedum.
  • Succulents come in a wide range of colors, sizes, and leaf shapes
  • Some varieties flower seasonally, producing bright, attractive blooms.

Benefits:

  • They purify indoor air and release oxygen, improving room quality.
  • Easy to grow and maintain, making them ideal for beginners.
  • Used in decor and landscaping due to their striking appearance.
  • Aloe Vera, a succulent, has medicinal properties (e.g., for burns and skin care).

Care Instructions:

  • Place in bright, indirect sunlight; at least 6 hours of light per day.
  • Water sparingly—only when the soil is completely dry (usually once every 1–2 weeks).
  • Use well-draining soil and pots with drainage holes to prevent root rot.
  • Avoid humidity and overwatering, which can harm the plant.
